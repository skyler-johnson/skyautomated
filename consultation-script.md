# Sky Automated — Free Consultation Script

## The Goal
Not to sell. To understand their business and show them you get it.
The sale happens naturally when they realize you know their problems better than they do.

---

## Opening (2 min)

"Thanks for taking the time — I'll keep this to about 30 minutes.

I'm not here to pitch you anything today. I just want to understand your business and show you a few things that could help. If it makes sense to work together, great. If not, no pressure at all."

**Why this works:** Takes the pressure off immediately. They relax. They talk more.

---

## Discovery Questions (10-15 min)

Ask these naturally — it's a conversation, not an interview.

### About Their Business
- "How long have you been running [business name]?"
- "How do most of your customers find you right now?"
- "What does a typical week look like for you?"

### Finding the Pain
- "What's the most time-consuming part of your day that isn't actually doing [their work]?"
- "Do you ever miss calls or messages when you're busy on a job?"
- "How do you handle follow-ups with customers after a job is done?"
- "Do you get Google reviews? How do you get them?"
- "Have you ever lost a customer to a competitor — do you know why?"

### The Website Question (if they don't have one)
- "When someone searches for [their service] in [city], do you show up?"
- "If a potential customer Googled you right now, what would they find?"

### The Automation Question
- "If you could remove one headache from your day, what would it be?"
- "Do you have a way to automatically follow up with leads, or is that all manual?"

---

## The Reveal (5-10 min)

After listening, summarize what you heard and show them the solution.

**Formula:** "So what I'm hearing is [their problem]. A lot of businesses I work with have the same issue. Here's what we typically do..."

### Example responses:

**If they have no website:**
> "Right now, when someone searches for you, they either can't find you or they find a competitor instead. We'd build you a professional site that shows up on Google, looks great on mobile, and has a contact form that notifies you instantly when someone reaches out."

**If they're missing follow-ups:**
> "So right now, if someone calls and you miss it, or fills out a form — what happens? Usually nothing, right? We set up an automatic text or email that goes out within 5 minutes. Studies show responding in under 5 minutes makes you 9x more likely to win the job."

**If they're drowning in scheduling:**
> "We can set up an online booking system so customers can schedule themselves — you just get a notification. No more phone tag."

**If they want more reviews:**
> "After every job, we automatically text your customer a review request. Most people are happy to leave one — they just forget to. Automation fixes that."

---

## Handling Objections

**"I can't afford it right now."**
> "I get that. That's actually why we structured it the way we did — the upfront cost is a one-time thing, and the monthly is low enough that one extra customer a month more than covers it. Want me to show you the math?"

**"I need to think about it."**
> "Totally fair. What's the main thing holding you back? Is it the cost, the timing, or something else? I'd rather address it now than leave you hanging."

**"I already have someone handling my website."**
> "Good to know — how's that working for you? Are you getting leads from it?" *(Most will say no or that it's outdated.)*

**"I'm not really tech-savvy."**
> "That's literally why we exist. You don't touch any of it — we handle everything. You just tell us what you need and we make it happen."

**"I don't think my customers use the internet."**
> "That's a common assumption but it's changing fast. Even for trades and local services, 80%+ of people Google a business before calling. If you're not showing up, they're calling someone else."

---

## The Close (2-3 min)

Don't hard sell. Give them a clear next step.

> "Based on what you've told me, I think [Starter / Growth / Full Auto] would be the best fit for you. It includes [X, Y, Z] and we'd have you live within [1-2 weeks].

> The investment is [price] upfront and [monthly] per month — no contracts, cancel anytime.

> Want to move forward, or do you have any other questions first?"

**If yes:** "Perfect. I'll send you a quick info form today — takes 10 minutes to fill out — and we'll get started."

**If maybe:** "No rush. Here's my card / here's the website. Think it over and reach out whenever you're ready. I'll follow up in a few days."

---

## After the Call

- Send a follow-up text or email within 2 hours:
  > "Hey [Name], great meeting you today! Here's the link to get started whenever you're ready: [link] — feel free to reach out with any questions."

- If no response in 3 days, follow up once:
  > "Hey [Name], just checking in — any questions I can answer for you?"

- After that, leave it. Don't chase. Move to the next prospect.

---

## Key Mindset

You're not a salesperson. You're a problem solver.
Listen more than you talk. The best consultation feels like a helpful conversation, not a pitch.
When they feel understood, they say yes.
